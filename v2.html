Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets is the second book in the Harry Potter series, written by J. K. Rowling.


Harry Potter has a busy summer: he spends a terrible birthday, receives sinister warnings from a house elf named Dobby and runs away from the Dusley family in his friend Ron's flying car.


At Hogwarts, a new school year begins, and Harry hears some strange whispers in the empty corridors. Then there are mysterious attacks - Dobby's dark predictions seem to be coming true. In the school, things are going crazy, the children from the Mageami families, those with the Sangegros start to be attacked by a beast that petrifies them. Harry, Ron and Hermione find out that the beast is a basilisk, a snake that kills you just by looking into your eyes.


Harry finds many clues until he manages to open the Chamber of Secrets using the Heptomite (the tongue of snakes) and finds the basilisk's abode, where he meets Doddle and Ron's younger sister Ginny. He learns that Doddle had used the diary and through him Ginny, so so that he returns to human form, he being in reality Lord Deathly Hallows (Voldemort), that wizard that everyone fears.


Finally, with the help of Joben and Fawkes (Dumbledore's phoenix) Harry defeats the basilisk and destroys Tom thus saving Ginny. Then, as we know, he will return to his aunt's house and will endure many evils during the vacation.

Harry Potter

The Harry Potter volumes form a very popular series around the world; they belong to the fantasy genre and were written by the British author J. K. Rowling. The books deal with a wizarding world, with the protagonist being a young wizard named Harry Potter, along with his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger.
