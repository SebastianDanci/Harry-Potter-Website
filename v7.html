Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows topped the bestseller lists just hours after the book's release date was announced on February 1, 2007


After Dumbledore's death, Voldemort continues to gain support. When Harry turns seventeen, the protection provided by his mother and his aunt and uncle's house is broken, and he flees to the Burrow with his friends (many of whom use Polyjuice Potion to impersonate Harry so as to mislead any Death Eater, who follow them).


They are attacked, but the Burrow is reached safely. Harry, Ron and Hermione know they cannot return to Hogwarts School for their seventh year, instead they complete Dumbledore's mission: to hunt down and destroy Voldemort's remaining Horcruxes, objects in which he has hidden parts of his soul, in order to be immortal. They have isolated themselves to ensure the protection of their friends and their families." They have little knowledge of the remaining horcruxes, except for the possibility that two are objects belonging to the founders of Hogwarts Rowena Ravenclaw, Helga Hufflepuff, and a third may be Nagini, the snake of Voldemort.


Locating the objects of the two founders is difficult, and Nagini is supposed to be with Voldemort. As they search for the Horcruxes, the trio learn more about Dumbledore's past. Bill Weasley and Fleur Delacour are married, but the wedding is interrupted by the news that Voldemort has taken over the Ministry of Magic. Harry, Ron and Hermione flee to London and arrive at Complete House No. 12, where they find out with the help of Kreacher where Salazar Viper's locket is. They successfully retrieve this Horcrux by infiltrating the Ministry of Magic and stealing it from Dolores Umbridge. Under the influence of the evil object and the stress of being on the run, Ron leaves Harry and Hermione.


They, after a long time spent hiding in the country, decide to travel to Godric's Hollow, Harry's birthplace and the place where his parents died, in Godric's Hollow they meet the magical historian Bathilda Bagshot, who turns out to be Nagini in disguise and attack them. They escape into the Forest of Dean, the mysterious part begins when a silver deer (a patronus) leads Harry to the Godric Gryffindor Sword at the bottom of a frozen lake, one of the few objects capable of destroying a Horcrux. When Harry tries to retrieve the sword from the lake, the horcrux tries to kill him. Ron reappears, fixed on the tank for Harry saving him, later using his sword to destroy the locket. Ron was not received by Hermione as warmly as Harry, she started yelling at him. After resuming their search, the trio encounters in several cases a strange symbol that was also seen around the neck of an eccentric wizard named Xenophilius Lovegood. In the end, they decide to pay Xenophilius Lovegood a visit, he tells them that he represents the 3 mythical Talismans of Death.


The talismans are three sacred objects: the Resurrection Stone, with the power to summon the dead to the world of the living; The Elder Wand, an unparalleled wand, as well as the supreme Invisibility Cloak. Harry learns that Voldemort is looking for the Elder Wand, but is unaware of the talismans and their meaning. The trio are captured and taken to Reassurance Manor, where Bellatrix Lestrange tortures Hermione. Harry and Ron encounter Luna Lovegood, Ollivander, Dean Thomas, and also Griphook held in the cellar. They all manage to escape and flee to Shell Cottage (Bill and Fleur's home), with Dobby's help, but at the cost of the house elf's life. Harry learns that Voldemort has robbed Dumbledore's tomb and taken the Shock Wand, but he decides to focus on Horcruxes instead of Hallows. With Griphook's help, they manage to break into Bellatrix's vault at Gringotts Wizarding Bank, which they believe contains another Horcrux. Their guess is correct and they manage to get hold of Helga Hufflepuff's cup and escape using a dragon. Harry learns that another Horcrux is hidden in Hogwarts, which is under the control of Severus Snape. Harry, Ron, Hermione enter the school through Hogsmeade (being saved by Aberforth Dumbledore, who explains more about Albus' story), with the help of the teachers Snape is expelled from the school. The Death Eaters and Voldemort storm the school, while Harry, Ron, Hermione search for the Horcrux related to Ravenclaw. At the same time, Ron and Hermione go to the Chamber of Secrets and destroy the cup.


The trio find Rowena Ravenclaw's tiara (the last Horcrux made from school founders' items) in the Chamber of Requirement. The Diadem is destroyed by the Cursed Fire summoned by Vincent Crabbe in a desperate attempt to kill Harry, Ron, Hermione and sadly ends with his own death. Voldemort and his followers lay siege to Hogwarts. Harry, Ron, Hermione, their allies and various magical creatures fight to defend Hogwarts. Several of the school's protectors are killed in the first wave of battle, including Remus Lupin, Nymphadora Tonks, and Fred Weasley. Voldemort kills Severus Snape because he believes that this will make him the true master of the Elder Wand. Harry discovers while watching the memories that Snape gives him on his deathbed that he sees that Voldemort unknowingly made Harry into a Horcrux when he attacked him as a child, and Harry must die for Voldemort to be able to be killed. These memoirs also confirm Snape's unwavering loyalty to Dumbledore and that his role as a double agent against Voldemort was decided when Voldemort killed Lily Evans who was Harry's mother and great love Snape's.

Harry Potter

The Harry Potter volumes form a very popular series around the world; they belong to the fantasy genre and were written by the British author J. K. Rowling. The books deal with a wizarding world, with the protagonist being a young wizard named Harry Potter, along with his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger.
