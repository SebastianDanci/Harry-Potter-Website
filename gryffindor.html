Gryffindor

Features

Founder: Godric Gryffindor

House colors: Scarlet and gold

Animal: Lion

Main feature: Bravery

Leader: Minerva McGonagall

Spirit: Nearly Headless Nick

Common room: Gryffindor Tower

Details

The House of Scouts is one of the four houses of the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, from the Harry Potter novels created by the British writer J. K. Rowling. The house was founded by Godric Gryffindor and is led by Professor Minerva McGonagall. Its specific colors are red and gold. Only brave, talented and pure-hearted children enter this House.


After many years in which the Scouts lost the House Cup to the Vipers, Harry Potter through his actions manages to bring the Cup back to the home of the brave Scouts. The common room is located in the Tower of the Scouts. The specific animal is the lion. Godric's legacy is the Sword of Gryffindor, which is the only memento of a founder that was not injected with Dark Magic by Voldemort, unlike Slytherin's locket, Ravenclaw's tiara, and Hufflepuff's cup. The sword helped destroy Riddle's Ring, Slytherin's Locket, and Nagini's by being injected with Basilisk venom, which can destroy Horcruxes.

Harry Potter

The Harry Potter volumes form a very popular series around the world; they belong to the fantasy genre and were written by the British author J. K. Rowling. The books deal with a wizarding world, with the protagonist being a young wizard named Harry Potter, along with his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger.
