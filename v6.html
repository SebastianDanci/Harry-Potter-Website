Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince is the sixth book in the Harry Potter series, written by J. K. Rowling.


Summer is in full swing, but an unusual fog for this season knocks on the windows. Harry is in his room, waiting for Professor Albus Dumbledore to visit in person, but not packing. One of the last times he saw the headmaster was when he was having a fierce duel with Lord Deathly Hallows, and Harry couldn't believe that Professor Dumbledore would actually show up in the Dursleys' house.


Why are they visiting him now? What is so important that it can't wait until Harry returns to Hogwarts in a few weeks? Harry's sixth year at Hogwarts has already begun in a strange way, as the Locked and Wizarding worlds begin to intertwine. J. K. Rowling designs the latest adventures of Harry Potter during his sixth year at Hogwarts with consummate craftsmanship and in a way that takes your breath away.


When Harry arrives at Hogwarts, Dumbledore begins to show him his memories, which later link together and give Harry valuable clues about Horcruxes (objects in which Voldemort stored a part of his soul in order to survive). However, Hogwarts is hiring a new Potions teacher, Horace Slughorn, Snape being in Defense Against the Dark Arts.


Slughorn also gives Harry a memory, a memory that helps him find out what Horcruxes are, and for his subject, Harry uses an old book with the help of which he will shine in the subject of Potions. But there are some instructions written on the book by the so-called "Half-Pure Prince", who later turns out to be none other than Snape. Harry later learns that he has already destroyed a horcrux in his second year in the Chamber of Secrets. He sets out with Dumbledore in search of the horcruxes, but he dies when he returns, as the Death Eaters break into the school and attack the teachers and students, he is killed by Snape. Harry witnesses all the goings-on, but of course under his Invisibility Cloak


Harry Potter

The Harry Potter volumes form a very popular series around the world; they belong to the fantasy genre and were written by the British author J. K. Rowling. The books deal with a wizarding world, with the protagonist being a young wizard named Harry Potter, along with his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger.
