Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban is the third volume of the Harry Potter novel series, written by J.K.Rowling.


In this volume, Harry meets for the first time Remus Lupine, the new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher, and Sirius Black, considered by the entire wizarding world to be a notorious criminal - 13-14 years ago he had mass-murdered a lot of "locked " including a wizard who tried to stop him, an act thought to have been caused by Voldemort's disappearance.


The fact that he had escaped from Azkaban is such important news that it was also transmitted over the information systems of the locked! But when he enters Hogwarts despite the many precautions taken by the school's headmaster and teachers, he turns out not to be as bloody as he seemed. Later we will even find out that he is the one who gave Harry the "Lightning" broom as a gift for Christmas.


Harry meets him after going through Bully Willow to help Ron. Sirius, who is actually Harry's godfather himself, only wants revenge on Peter Pettigrew, the real person responsible for the death of Harry's parents, James and Lily Potter, and who is not as dead as he has been led to believe so far that he was turned into Ron Weasley's rat for 12 years.


Harry Potter

The Harry Potter volumes form a very popular series around the world; they belong to the fantasy genre and were written by the British author J. K. Rowling. The books deal with a wizarding world, with the protagonist being a young wizard named Harry Potter, along with his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger.
